CACHAPA

DESCRIPTION

Type of tortilla prepared with corn, milk or water, sugar and salt, similar to a pancake. It has a sweet taste

INGREDIENTS

  • 450 grams of corn kernels (can be frozen or canned)
  • ½ cup of milk
  • 2 whole eggs
  • 1 tablespoon wheat flour (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons of corn flour
  • 1 spoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • salt to your taste
  • Sliced ​​mozzarella cheese to fill the cachapas (since where we live we don't find “e'mano cheese”)

Steps to follow

  1. In a frying pan, put a teaspoon of butter the size of a walnut. This amount is enough to make a cachapa.
  2. Wait for the butter to sizzle a little and add a ladleful of the cachapa dough. Let it cook over medium heat until you see that bubbles appear in the dough, or holes form, like in pancakes.
  3. When the bubbles described in the previous point are observed, turn the cachapa so that it cooks on the other side. Each cachapa is cooked for more or less 3 or 4 minutes on each side, depending on its size.
  4. To finish, place half a wheel of hand cheese (failing that, mozarella) on the cachapa and close it with the other half so that the cheese melts with the heat of the cachapa itself.
  5. Cachapas are served accompanied by coffee, juice or whatever drink you like the most, to accompany breakfast or a snack.
